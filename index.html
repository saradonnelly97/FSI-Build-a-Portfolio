a not-so-quick story that may help you understand who i am

TIGERHOLE

To me, Earthbound (Mother 2) is probably one of the best games of all time. Because of Earthbound, I've gained a really strange sense of humor, which is reflected particularly strongly in my artwork. I enjoy how complex even simple doodles can be. They look like they're easy to draw, but even then, the most simple doodles have a good bit of thought and muscle memory incorporated into them. My goal is to execute my doodles almost as effortless as they look (because they do take me a bit of trial and error before they actually look good!)

For example; These paintings took a little over four hours to make each. The original painting was the Tiger. I haven't painted using acrylics in some time, let alone ever tried a 'line-less' painting style. My first step was painting the 4inx4in canvas Candy Pink. After the background layer dried, I sat down and drew out my funky little tiger in pencil. From there, things got a bit tricky. I went straight for a layer of orange, getting the paint as flat as possible without laying down big globs of paint. When I created pieces previously, I was so enamored by the texture of the paint alone that I would slap down the thickest gobs of paint I possibly could. Reach resulted in very long dry times as well as an expensive muddy mess. When I put down the initial layers, I left a significant amount of space between the paint and the pencil lines; I was too scared to ruin my beautiful drawing... With a leap of faith, I went for trusting the colors to work for themselves and painted over the sketch completely. After the base colors were established, I got a bit lost. What could I do to bring this painting all together? It came to me; I could draw on it. Following my move-in, I'd unearthed a pack of beautiful, Windsor-Newton watercolor pencils. The best way to bring any piece together is to make sure there is a proper amount of red, blue, and yellow. These three colors somehow manage to trick the mind into thinking a piece is complete. Don't quote me on that, but I remember my painting professor mentioning something about it very briefly in one of our first classes back in the day... Not only that, but I noticed that the pencils added a wonderful amount of texture to the painting as well. Once the shading was added, I added all the black details with a felt-tip art pen. And Voila! Tiger Hole was complete.

The next day, I realized I had two more 4inx4in canvas that I could paint, and honestly Tiger Hole looked a little lonely. I wanted to make another big cat, but I also knew that I wanted to make the paintings look like they were interacting. Like they belonged together. This time, I approached the painting a little differently.. I sketched my idea out on a piece of notebook paper before commiting it to the canvas. I was nervous that my next drawing wouldn't live up to the quirkiness of Tiger Hole. I'd never drawn or painted in the style that I had the day before and was left surprised I could make something so weird but cool. The sketch I came up with was just so perfect, I was a bit devastated and worried I wouldn't be able to free-hand the exact same sketch again. I came up with a brilliant idea; If I traced the sketch on the other side of the paper, I could re-draw the sketch onto the canvas using the trace! This proved to be an excellent solution, and I from there, I was able to follow the same steps I'd followed with Tiger Hole to create Tiger Hole 2. When I placed the paintings side by side, they sat beside each other, interacting more perfect than I could ever ask for. It was a success.

And then came the moment I was dreading. Fearing. I had one final canvas left. What could I possibly put on this canvas?? What if it doesn't fit in with the other two? What direction should this animal even face? Should I do another big cat or should I do a unicorn? I knew I at least wanted to use a blue canvas. Ok, well, the best creature I could do is probably a panther if I'm wanting these paintings to go together. What color should I make the panther? Black will be way too harsh and will take away from the other two paintings... There were so many questions I asked myself before I finally decided to paint a purple panther. And that's exactly what creating art is all about; it's another form of problem solving, a series of solutions you find only after an extensive session of trial and error. I made multiple sketches of this fella. I just could not get his face or his body to look right! After I finally figured out his silly face, his broad and strong shoulders, I had another problem I had to face. The tracing did not fit onto my 4inx4in canvas. I had two choices; to attempt to free-hand draw the panther onto the canvas OR drag the drawing paper along the canvas as I traced to force it to fit onto the canvas. Aaaaaaannnddd the second choice proved to be the best answer because I didn't want to sit down and draw another panther. This guy was just too perfect. Somehow, I managed to paint the panther much quicker than the other two; Tiger Hole 2 taking me the longest (I had some unfortunate issues with what shade of yellow I wanted and had to re-do the cheetah a couple times!) The blue and red shading fit on perfectly, and I had to add on a lot more definition with the pencils than my other two drawings. I was afraid that because of this, the painting wouldn't fit in with the other two. I waited for it to dry and then...
